Target CWT at Design WBT

What it is

The Cold Water Temperature (°C) the tower must reach at exactly your Design Wet Bulb Temperature. It anchors the 100% performance curve to the contractual guarantee.

When to use

After entering a 100% Flow / 100% Range safety margin, subtract that margin from the Design CWT.

Example: CWT 33°C − Margin 1.2°C → Target 31.8°C

Then click Fit Curve — the engine recalculates Const C so the 100% curve exactly hits 33°C at Design WBT.

⚠ Why this matters

Without this correction, a safety margin shifts the 100% curve upward — making the tower appear to fail at the design point, which is contractually unacceptable.

Low WBT Rotation (at 20°C)

What it is

A linearly-tapering safety tilt on the cold end of the curve. The full offset value is added at 20°C WBT and tapers smoothly to zero at the Design WBT. The design point is never affected.

When to use

When you need conservative protection at low ambient temperatures where cooling tower behavior is less predictable and measurement uncertainty is higher.

🔬 Engineering Note

At low WBTs, the air-water enthalpy driving force is at its maximum — but turbulence and flow irregularities also peak. This tilt quantifies engineering caution in that uncertain zone.
